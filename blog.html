Places to visit in Baku

October 11, 2022

Walled City (Icheri Sheher)

The Icheri Sheher, which is called the "Old City" by the local people, is a region that travelers must include in their lists of places to visit in Baku, as it preserves many historical structures. Inhabited since the Paleolithic era, the region was recognized as one of the World Heritage Sites in Danger in 2003, following its inclusion on the UNESCO World Heritage List in 2000. In the cobbled streets of the old town there is a historic building of Hellenic.

Shirvanshahs Palace

The Palace of the Shirvanshahs, considered among the most important examples of local architecture, was built during the reign of Ibrahim Halilullah, the king of the Shirvanshahs.

Maiden Tower

Maiden Tower The 27-meter Maiden Tower, thought to be an ancient Zoroastrian temple, was built in the 12th century. The tower, which tells many stories about its history, was used as a lighthouse in the 18th and 19th centuries. The 8-story building in the southeastern part of Inside City now houses exhibition stands.

Ateshgah Fire Temple

The Fire Temple, or "Fire Temple", is located in the city of Surakhani, 30 kilometers from the center of Baku. The first structure of the temple, which is one of the 3 remaining Fire-worshipping temples, was built in 1713, and the central shrine was built in 1810. Formerly important places of worship for Zoroastrians, some rooms are now used as museums.

Seaside Boulevard

Baku Boulevard opened in 1909, runs parallel to the coast of Baku. At the beginning of the century, the region where the oil merchants living in Baku lived was later named Seaside National Park and protected. The boulevard, created by filling the sea, was expanded after gaining independence and turned into an attraction center with museums, monuments and entertainment centers.
